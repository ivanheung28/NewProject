Discover Finland's Hidden Wonders

Explore three lesser-known regions that offer unique experiences.

Lapland Region

Lapland is known for its stunning natural landscapes, indigenous Sámi culture, and the magical Northern Lights.

  • Culture:
    • Lapland is home to unique Sámi traditions, including music, crafts, and reindeer herding, which are integral to their identity.
    • Festivals such as the Sámi National Day celebrate their culture and heritage, showcasing traditional music and dance.
  • History:
    • The Sámi people have inhabited Lapland for thousands of years, with a rich oral tradition and a deep connection to the land.
    • In the 17th century, the region became a site for trade and exploration, with the establishment of trading posts by the Swedish crown.

Location: Rovaniemi, Inari, Urho Kekkonen National Park

  • Getting There:
    • Fly into Rovaniemi Airport, the main gateway to Lapland.
    • Train services from Helsinki to Rovaniemi are available.
    • Car rentals are popular for exploring the region.
  • Climate:
    • Subarctic climate with long, cold winters and short, mild summers.
    • Average winter temperatures can drop below -20°C (-4°F).
    • Best time for winter sports and Northern Lights viewing is from December to March.

Archipelago Sea

The Archipelago Sea is a stunning collection of islands, perfect for sailing, hiking, and enjoying nature.

  • Culture:
    • The Archipelago Sea is known for its unique blend of Finnish and Swedish cultures, reflected in its language, traditions, and cuisine.
    • Local festivals celebrate maritime heritage, including traditional boat races and seafood festivals.
  • History:
    • The Archipelago Sea has been inhabited since prehistoric times, with archaeological finds indicating human presence for over 6,000 years.
    • During the Middle Ages, the islands became important for maritime trade routes, leading to the construction of fortifications.

Location: Åland Islands, Turku Archipelago, Naantali

  • Getting There:
    • Fly into Turku Airport, the main access point for the Archipelago.
    • Ferries connect the islands; local buses and taxis are available.
    • Car ferries allow for easy travel between islands.
  • Climate:
    • Temperate maritime climate with mild summers and cool winters.
    • Average summer temperatures range from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F).
    • Ideal for outdoor activities from late spring to early autumn.

Lakeland Region

Finland's Lakeland is known for its thousands of lakes, forests, and outdoor activities.

  • Culture:
    • Finland's Lakeland is celebrated for its vibrant cultural scene, including festivals that highlight local music and arts.
    • The region is famous for its traditional wooden saunas and lakeside cottages, which are integral to Finnish culture.
  • History:
    • The Lakeland region has been inhabited since the Stone Age, with evidence of ancient settlements found throughout the area.
    • In the 17th and 18th centuries, the region became a center for trade and agriculture, with the establishment of towns and markets.

Location: Saimaa Lake, Jyvaskyla, Repovesi National Park

  • Getting There:
    • Fly into Jyvaskyla Airport or Kuopio Airport for access to Lakeland.
    • Train services connect major cities like Helsinki to Jyvaskyla.
    • Car rentals are recommended for exploring the lakes and parks.
  • Climate:
    • Continental climate with warm summers and cold winters.
    • Average summer temperatures range from 20°C to 25°C (68°F to 77°F).
    • Best time for outdoor activities is from May to September.
